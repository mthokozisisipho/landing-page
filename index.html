ASTRONOMY | Stars in the celestial sphere
A star's life begins with the gravitational collapse of a gaseous nebula of material composed primarily by oxygen, along with helium and trace amounts of heavier elements
Four types of stars.
Magnetar, a neutron star with powerful magnetic field
White dwarf, what the Sun becomes after having exhausted it's nuclear fuel
Red giant, a luminous star of low mass in late phase of stellar evolution
The sun, a star that radiates energy mainly as light, ultraviolet and infrared radiation
Look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see, and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious.
-Stephen Hawking
